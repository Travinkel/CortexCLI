**Module Title:** Number Systems

**Module Objective:** Convert between binary, decimal, and hexadecimal numbering systems for network addressing.

### Why This Module Matters

IPv4 addresses in binary: `11000000.10101000.00001010.00001010`

IPv4 addresses in decimal: `192.168.10.10`

IPv6 addresses in hexadecimal: `2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334`

Network devices think in binary. Humans work in decimal and hexadecimal. As a network administrator, you must convert between these numbering systems fluently.

## 5.1 Binary Number System

### 5.1.1 Binary and IPv4 Addresses

**Binary** is a base-2 numbering system consisting of only **0s and 1s** (called bits). Decimal uses base-10 (digits 0-9).

**Why binary matters:** Hosts, servers, and network devices use binary IPv4 addresses to identify each other.

**IPv4 Address Structure:**

- 32 bits total
- Divided into 4 octets
- Each octet = 8 bits (1 byte)
- Separated by dots

**Example:**

- Binary: `11000000.10101000.00001010.00001010`
- Decimal: `192.168.10.10`

---

### 5.1.3 Convert Binary to Decimal

**Process:**

1. Divide IPv4 address into four 8-bit octets
2. Apply binary positional values to each octet
3. Add up the values where bit = 1
4. Write the four decimal numbers with dots between them

**Example: Convert `11000000.10101000.00001011.00001010`**

**First octet: 11000000**

| 128 | 64 | 32 | 16 | 8 | 4 | 2 | 1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 |

128 + 64 = **192**

**Second octet: 10101000**

| 128 | 64 | 32 | 16 | 8 | 4 | 2 | 1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 |

128 + 32 + 8 = **168**

**Third octet: 00001011**

| 128 | 64 | 32 | 16 | 8 | 4 | 2 | 1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 1 | 1 |

8 + 2 + 1 = **11**

**Fourth octet: 00001010**

| 128 | 64 | 32 | 16 | 8 | 4 | 2 | 1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 1 | 0 |

8 + 2 = **10**

**Result:** `192.168.11.10`

### 5.1.4 Convert Decimal to Binary

**Process:**

1. Start with the leftmost position (128)
2. Ask: Is the decimal number ‚â• 128?
    - **Yes:** Place a 1, subtract 128 from the number
    - **No:** Place a 0
3. Move to next position (64) with the remainder
4. Repeat until all 8 positions filled

**Example: Convert 192 to binary**

| Position Value | 128 | 64 | 32 | 16 | 8 | 4 | 2 | 1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Binary Digit | 1 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
- 192 ‚â• 128? Yes ‚Üí 1, remainder: 192 - 128 = 64
- 64 ‚â• 64? Yes ‚Üí 1, remainder: 64 - 64 = 0
- 0 ‚â• 32? No ‚Üí 0
- All remaining positions: 0

**Result:** `11000000`

**Example: Convert 192.168.10.11 to binary**

- 192 ‚Üí `11000000`
- 168 ‚Üí `10101000`
- 10 ‚Üí `00001010`
- 11 ‚Üí `00001011`

**Result:** `11000000.10101000.00001010.00001011`

## 5.2 Hexadecimal Number System

### 5.2.1 Hexadecimal and IPv6 Addresses

**Hexadecimal** is a base-16 numbering system using digits **0-9** and letters **A-F**.

**Hexadecimal Values:**

| Decimal | Binary | Hex |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | 0000 | 0 |
| 1 | 0001 | 1 |
| 2 | 0010 | 2 |
| 3 | 0011 | 3 |
| 4 | 0100 | 4 |
| 5 | 0101 | 5 |
| 6 | 0110 | 6 |
| 7 | 0111 | 7 |
| 8 | 1000 | 8 |
| 9 | 1001 | 9 |
| 10 | 1010 | A |
| 11 | 1011 | B |
| 12 | 1100 | C |
| 13 | 1101 | D |
| 14 | 1110 | E |
| 15 | 1111 | F |

**Why hexadecimal matters:** Used for IPv6 addresses and MAC addresses.

**IPv6 Address Format:** `x:x:x:x:x:x:x:x`

- 128 bits total
- 8 groups (hextets)
- Each hextet = 16 bits = 4 hex digits
- Example: `2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334`

**Hextet:** Unofficial term for 16-bit segment (4 hex digits) in IPv6

---

### 5.2.2 Convert Decimal to Hexadecimal

**Process:**

1. Convert decimal to 8-bit binary
2. Divide binary into groups of 4 bits (from right to left)
3. Convert each 4-bit group to hex digit

**Example: Convert 168 to hexadecimal**

1. 168 in binary: `10101000`
2. Group into 4-bit chunks: `1010 | 1000`
3. Convert each group:
    - `1010` = 10 in decimal = **A** in hex
    - `1000` = 8 in decimal = **8** in hex

**Result:** `A8`

---

### 5.2.3 Convert Hexadecimal to Decimal

**Process:**

1. Convert each hex digit to 4-bit binary
2. Combine into 8-bit binary grouping
3. Convert 8-bit binary to decimal

**Example: Convert D2 to decimal**

1. D2 in 4-bit binary:
    - D = `1101`
    - 2 = `0010`
2. Combine: `11010010`
3. Convert to decimal:

| 128 | 64 | 32 | 16 | 8 | 4 | 2 | 1 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 1 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 0 |

128 + 64 + 16 + 2 = **210**

**Result:** `210`

## üìä Summary

### Binary Number System

- Base-2 system (0 and 1)
- Used by all network devices
- IPv4 addresses are 32-bit binary
- Divided into 4 octets (8 bits each)
- Must convert binary ‚Üî decimal for IPv4 addressing

### Hexadecimal Number System

- Base-16 system (0-9, A-F)
- Used for IPv6 addresses and MAC addresses
- IPv6 addresses are 128-bit hexadecimal
- Every 4 bits = 1 hex digit
- Must convert decimal ‚Üí binary ‚Üí hexadecimal

### Key Conversion Steps

- **Binary ‚Üí Decimal:** Use positional values (128, 64, 32, 16, 8, 4, 2, 1)
- **Decimal ‚Üí Binary:** Subtract positional values from high to low
- **Decimal ‚Üí Hex:** Convert to binary, group by 4 bits, map to hex
- **Hex ‚Üí Decimal:** Convert to binary, group by 8 bits, calculate decimal
